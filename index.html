Week 2
For this week's update I will be talking about myself and how I became passionate about music.
My love for playing music comes from an early indoctrination into the music world. My mother is a signer songwriter,
my uncle is a studio guitarist, my grandmother sang jazz tunes while my grandfather backed her up with piano.
From a young age I would watch my family perform together and I knew that I wanted to perform like them.

At 10 years old I had saved up enough money from mowing lawns to purchase my first guitar.
It was an all black Epiphone Les Paul Special II. This guitar started my passion for not just playing guitar but building them as well.
After many years of playing and tinkering with guitars I finally decided to fully build one on my own. This is the first instrument I ever built, and it was built for a band I had been playing with for a few years.
It is a PJ Fender style bass with a rosewood neck, Duncan PJ set with a blender knob configuration.
I could write pages on the building process of this bass, but I will leave some links that helped me with my first build

For first time builders I would recommend building from a set of parts instead of fabricating them yourself.
My favorite site for parts would have to be Warmoth Custom Guitar and Bass Parts
Another great resource for building is Stewmac's Wiring diagrams.
Finally, the resource I used the most during my first build was YouTube.
Any question I had during my build was usually answered by a YouTube video.
